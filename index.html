Where to Go

Hand-picked spots for coffee lovers, art heads, and flaneurs. Focused around the 1st, 2nd, 9th, and 20th districts.

☕ Coffee 2nd

Jonas Reindl Coffee Roasters

Specialty roaster with a minimalist space. Excellent filter coffee and single origins. A local favorite in Leopoldstadt.

Wehlistraße 18, 1020

☕ Coffee 9th

Café Couture

Tiny, beautiful third-wave spot in Alsergrund. The latte art is impeccable, the vibe calm and creative. Try the flat white.

Garnisongasse 18, 1090

☕ Coffee 7th

Kaffeefabrik

No-frills specialty coffee with obsessive attention to brewing. Standing-room only, take your espresso like a local.

Favoritenstraße 4-6, 1040

☕ Coffee 1st

Café Central

Iconic Viennese coffeehouse under vaulted ceilings. Yes, it's touristy — but the architecture and Apfelstrudel are worth it once.

Herrengasse 14, 1010

☕ Coffee 4th

CoffeePirates

Cozy specialty coffee shop near Naschmarkt. Great espresso drinks, friendly baristas, and a cool indie vibe.

Spittelberggasse 17, 1070

🍳 Brunch 2nd

Harvest

Plant-forward brunch with seasonal bowls, homemade granola, and gorgeous plating. The kind of place you linger for hours.

Karmeliterplatz 1, 1020

🍳 Brunch 9th

Vollpension

Grandmas bake the cakes here. Seriously. A social enterprise café with unbeatable homemade Austrian pastries and charm.

Schleifmühlgasse 16, 1040

Eclectic, colorful brunch spot in Neubau. Creative dishes, amazing Shakshuka, and strong weekend brunch energy.

Middle-Eastern meets Viennese brunch. Beautiful interiors, killer Eggs Benedict, and one of the best brunches in the 2nd district.

Kunsthistorisches Museum

One of the world's greatest art museums. Bruegel, Vermeer, Caravaggio — in a building that's itself a masterpiece. Don't miss the cupola café.

Maria-Theresien-Platz, 1010

🎨 Art & Museum 3rd

Belvedere

Klimt's "The Kiss" lives here. Stunning Baroque palace with incredible art nouveau collection and gorgeous gardens.

Prinz-Eugen-Straße 27, 1030

mumok

Museum of Modern Art in the MuseumsQuartier. Bold contemporary exhibitions in a dark basalt cube. Always surprising.

Museumsplatz 1, 1070

🎨 Art & Museum 1st

Albertina Modern

Contemporary art in the Karlsplatz area. Rotating exhibitions of post-war and contemporary art in a beautifully redesigned space.

Your neighborhood park! Baroque gardens, a porcelain factory, and WWII flak towers. Perfect for a morning walk — 10 min from the Airbnb.

Vienna's Gothic heart. Climb the 343 steps of the south tower for panoramic views. The tiled roof is mesmerizing from below.

Getting Around

Transport

Vienna is incredibly walkable, and the public transport is world-class. Here's what you need to know.

✈ Airport → Airbnb

Vienna Airport (VIE) is ~30 min from the city. Best options:

CAT (City Airport Train) — 16 min to Wien Mitte, then U4 to Friedensbrücke (closest U-Bahn). €14.90 one-way.
S7 train — Cheaper (€4.40 with city zone), ~25 min to Wien Mitte. Same transfer.
Taxi/Bolt — Fixed fare ~€36-39 to the 20th. Easiest with luggage.

🚇 U-Bahn & Trams

Vienna's metro is fast, clean, and runs until ~00:30 (24h on Fri/Sat nights!).

Closest station: Friedensbrücke (U4, green line) — 12 min walk from your Airbnb. Also Dresdner Straße (U6).
48-hour Vienna pass: €14.10 — unlimited metro, tram, bus. Absolutely worth it for a weekend.

Tram lines 2 and 5 run close to the Airbnb and connect to the Ringstraße.

🚶 Walking Distances

Your Airbnb on Brigittenauer Lände is perfectly located for walking:

Augarten: 10 min 🌱
Danube Canal: 5 min 🌊
Schwedenplatz (center): 25 min 🚶
Karmeliterviertel (2nd): 20 min 🚶
Stephansdom: 30 min 🚶

Good to Know

Practical Tips

Little things that make a big difference.

🍴 Must-Try Foods

Wiener Schnitzel — must be veal, pounded thin, golden.
Sachertorte — chocolate cake with apricot jam.
Kaiserschmarrn — torn pancake with plum compote.
Topfenstrudel — sweet cheese strudel.
Tafelspitz — boiled beef, a Viennese classic.

💰 Tipping Etiquette

Round up or tip 5-10% at restaurants. Say the total you want to pay when handing over cash (e.g., "Stimmt so" = keep the change). At cafés, rounding up by a euro or two is standard.

💧 Tap Water

Vienna's tap water comes from Alpine springs and is excellent. Totally safe and delicious — bring a reusable bottle. In restaurants, ask for "Leitungswasser" (tap water).

🌎 Language

Almost everyone speaks English. But a few German phrases go a long way: "Grüß Gott" (hello), "Danke" (thanks), "Bitte" (please/you're welcome), "Zahlen bitte" (check please).

💳 Paying

Vienna is still somewhat cash-heavy. Many small cafés and Beisln prefer cash. ATMs (Bankomat) are everywhere. Cards work at larger restaurants and shops.

🌞 April Weather

Expect 10-18°C. Layers are key — mornings can be cool, afternoons warm. A light jacket and a scarf will serve you well. Rain is possible, so pack a small umbrella.

🛒 Sunday Shopping

Most shops are closed on Sundays. Plan your vintage shopping and Naschmarkt visit for Saturday. Restaurants and cafés are open.

🎫 Naschmarkt Tips

Go Saturday morning for the flea market. Start from the Kettenbrückengasse end. Eat there — the Turkish and Middle Eastern stalls are incredible. Arrive before 11 AM to beat crowds.
